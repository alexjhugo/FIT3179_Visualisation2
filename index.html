TOKYO 2020 SUMMER OLYMPICS

The Tokyo 2020 Summer Olympics is an international sports event that was long awaited by many countries, since the last summer olympic games in Brazil, 2016. It was the fourth olympic games to be held in Japan, set in their capital city of Tokyo. It was also unfortunately delayed by a year due to the emergence of the COVID-19 Pandemic that caused mass lockdowns around the world. However, the games eventually took place and through this visualisation, we'll discuss about some of the event's key highlights.

USA vs China

Through the map above, we can see that the United States of America (USA) and the People's Republic of China (China) are the top two countries that obtained the most number of medals in the olympic games. They are also the top countries in terms of the number of medals in their respective regions of North America and Asia. The USA may have a considerably higher amount of total medals than China. However, China is not far behind at all as it is only a Gold medal behind the USA.

European Domination

Europe is the best region in terms of the overall medal count. It has a lot of countries within it that obtained medals, and significant portion of them are strong performers. These include a number of Western European countries such as Great Britain, France, Germany, Netherlands, and Italy. Russia (represented by the Russian Olympic Committee), a country that spans both Europe and Asia is also a solid performer. It won a total of 71 medals, 20 of which are Gold.
