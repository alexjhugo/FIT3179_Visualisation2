TOKYO 2020 SUMMER OLYMPICS

The Tokyo 2020 Summer Olympics is an international sports event that was long awaited by many countries, since the last summer olympic games in Brazil, 2016. It was the fourth olympic games to be held in Japan, set in their capital city of Tokyo. It was also unfortunately delayed by a year due to the emergence of the COVID-19 Pandemic that caused mass lockdowns around the world. However, the games eventually took place and through this visualisation, we'll discuss about some of the event's key highlights.

Number of Medals by Country

American Victory

The United States of America (US) is the top performing country in terms of the overall total number of medals. It has acquired a total of 113 medals. It is consequently the top country in terms of the medal count in the North American region, beating its neighbours, Canada and Mexico, by a large margin. In addition, it had also surpassed its rival superpower, China, in the overall number of medals. However, China is actually not far behind the US for the other medal categories and performance. This is shown through the other visualisations below.

European Domination

Europe is the best region in terms of the overall medal count. It has a lot of countries within it that obtained medals, and a significant portion of them are strong performers. These include a number of Western European countries such as Great Britain and Germany. To the East, we have Russia (represented by the Russian Olympic Committee), acquiring a total of 71 medals which is a significant portion of Europe's overall medal count. This places Europe as the top region in the world, in terms of the overall medal count.


Medal Type Distribution of the Top 10 Countries


The heatmap on the left shows the distribution of the medal types of the top ten performing countries. Through it, we can see that China and the US performed similarly in terms of their Gold medal count. We can also see that four Western European countries: the Netherlands, France, Germany, and Italy, performed similarly with one another in terms of Gold and Silver medals. As for the Bronze medals, they each performed somewhat differently with one another.

A similar pattern again applies to Great Britain and Russia. The two Countries obtained a similar amount of medals for the Gold and Bronze categories. The main factor differentiating the two is that Russia had won quite a higher amount of Silver medals than Great Britain. Despite their impressive performance, the two still got outclassed by Japan in terms of the Gold medal count. It seems that being the host country really has really boosted their athletes' morales, doesn't it?


Performance of Countries with Gold Medals

Superpower Competition

As mentioned previously, the US had beaten China in terms of the total medal count. However, they actually performed similarly in terms of the gold medals obtained, as shown from our heatmap above. In fact, China is actually performing better than the US when we look at their athletes to gold medal ratio. China has about 11 athletes for every gold medal obtained, while the US has roughly 16 athletes for every gold medal it won.

Small but Mighty

Kosovo is a state located in the Balkans, at Southern Europe. It has a small landmass and is landlocked by several countries. However, it performed very impressively in terms of the athlete to gold medal ratio. It obtained a ratio of 5 athletes for every gold medals won, despite only sending a few athletes to the games. Kosovo had proven to us that bigger countries don't necessarily outperform small countries, if we compare them using this ratio.


Athlete Gender Distribution in Popular Sport Disciplines

Through the bar chart on the right, it is evident that the majority of the sport discipline are dominated mostly by male athletes. This is especially true for disciplines such as Athletics, Swimming, and Football. Despite that, the number of male athletes is not significantly larger than the number of female athletes for most of the sport disciplines.

In fact, some of the sport disciplines are exclusively female only. These include Artistic Swimming and Rhythmic Gymnastics. They are both a sport discipline that is by tradition, only meant for female athletes. Thus, no male athletes are allowed to participate in them, which is reflected by our chart.

As for the sport disciplines, we can tell that Athlethics is the most participated one because it has the highest number of athletes for both genders. On the other hand, Trampoline Gymnastics has the least amount of athletes of both genders amongst the sport disciplines shown in the bar chart. It is also the least participated gymnastic sport discipline, when comparing to Artistic Gymnastics and Rhythmic Gymnastics.
